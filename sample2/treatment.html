What is Electrolysis ?

Electrolysis is a method of permanent hair removal that targets individual hair follicles using heat or chemicals to destroy the hair growth cells. During electrolysis, a small probe is inserted into the hair follicle, and either heat (thermolysis), chemical reaction (galvanic electrolysis), or a combination of both (blend method) is used to damage the follicle and prevent future hair growth. This process is repeated for each hair follicle, making it a highly precise and effective method for removing unwanted hair permanently. Electrolysis can be used on various areas of the body and is suitable for all skin and hair types.

What does Electrolysis work ?

Electrolysis works by targeting individual hair follicles and destroying their ability to grow hair permanently. The process typically involves the following steps:

  1. Insertion : A fine probe, comparable in diameter to the hair being treated, is inserted into the follicle.
  2. Application of current: Once inserted, a small electrical current, often heat-based (thermolysis), chemically driven (galvanic electrolysis), or a blend of both (blend method), is applied to the follicle.
  3. Destruction of hair follicle: The current destroys the hair growth cells within the follicle, halting further hair growth. This destruction can involve heat generation or chemical reactions.
  4. Removal of hair: Post-treatment, weakened or detached hair is removed using tweezers or similar tools.
  5. Repeat sessions: Given hair growth cycles, multiple sessions are typically necessary to treat all active follicles in a specific area. This ensures comprehensive permanent hair removal.

Electrolysis is a precise and effective method, suitable for various hair types and skin tones, commonly used on areas such as the face, underarms, bikini line, and legs to achieve enduring hair-free results.
